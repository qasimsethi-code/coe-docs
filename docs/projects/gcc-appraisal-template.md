# GCC Used-Car Appraisal Template

!!! success "Built — Standardized Templates for GCC and Non-GCC Models"

---

## Overview

The GCC Used-Car Appraisal Template project provides standardized vehicle appraisal templates that ensure consistency, completeness, and compliance across all COE pricing activities. The templates cover both GCC-specification and non-GCC-specification vehicles, with dedicated sections for the unique risk factors associated with each category.

---

## Template Structure

The appraisal templates follow a structured format designed to capture all data points required for a defensible valuation.

| Section | Content |
|---|---|
| **Vehicle Identification** | VIN, make, model, year, trim, GCC/non-GCC status |
| **Specification Verification** | Origin confirmation, trim matching, feature comparison |
| **Condition Assessment** | Mechanical inspection results, body condition, interior condition |
| **Accident & Damage History** | MOI records, structural damage assessment, repair history |
| **Market Benchmarking** | Retail listings, auction results, fleet monitor data |
| **Valuation Bands** | Recommended COE valuation ranges (retail, forecourt, liquidation) |
| **Risk Assessment** | Risk tier classification, penalty stack for non-GCC models |
| **Decision & Justification** | Final recommendation (ACQUIRE / NEGOTIATE / DECLINE) with reasoning |

---

## GCC vs. Non-GCC Differentiation

A critical aspect of the template design is the differentiation between GCC and non-GCC specification vehicles. Non-GCC vehicles carry additional risk factors that must be explicitly documented and accounted for in the valuation.

**Non-GCC Penalty Stack** includes factors such as warranty limitations, parts availability concerns, specification mismatches (e.g., cold-climate features in a Gulf market), and reduced resale demand. The template requires appraisers to document each applicable penalty and its impact on the final valuation.

---

## 3-Level Valuation Tables

The templates incorporate 3-level valuation tables that provide pricing at three tiers: optimistic (best-case), expected (most likely), and conservative (worst-case). This range-based approach gives decision-makers flexibility while ensuring that all scenarios are documented and justified.

---

## Impact

The standardized templates have improved consistency across the pricing team, reduced disputes with dealers by providing clear documentation of valuation logic, and created a defensible audit trail for every appraisal decision.
