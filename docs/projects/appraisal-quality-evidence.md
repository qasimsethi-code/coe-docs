# Appraisal Quality & Evidence Standardization

!!! success "Authored — Reducing Disputes and Improving Accountability"

## Overview

The Appraisal Quality & Evidence Standardization initiative aims to reduce disputes, improve estimator accountability, and increase dealer confidence through rigorous documentation standards. This project establishes the quality framework that underpins all appraisal activities within the COE.

---

## Objectives

| Objective | Description |
|---|---|
| **Reduce Disputes** | Minimize pricing disagreements with dealers through transparent, well-documented valuations |
| **Improve Accountability** | Establish clear standards for estimator documentation, ensuring every assessment is traceable |
| **Increase Dealer Confidence** | Build trust through consistent, professional appraisal outputs backed by evidence |

---

## Key Documents

The initiative is supported by several authored documents that define the quality standards.

### Damage vs. Actual Appraisal Analysis

This document provides a systematic framework for comparing estimated damage costs against actual repair outcomes. By tracking the variance between initial damage assessments and final repair costs, the COE can identify patterns of over- or under-estimation and calibrate its appraisal models accordingly.

### Standardized Photo & Evidence Checklist

The photo and evidence checklist defines the minimum documentation requirements for every vehicle appraisal. It specifies the required photo angles, lighting conditions, and detail shots needed to create a complete visual record of the vehicle's condition at the time of appraisal.

---

## Standards Framework

The quality framework establishes several key principles for appraisal documentation.

**Completeness** requires that every appraisal includes all mandatory data points, photos, and supporting documents. Incomplete appraisals are flagged for review before pricing decisions are made.

**Consistency** ensures that the same vehicle, assessed by different appraisers, would receive comparable documentation and valuation. The standardized templates and checklists enforce this consistency.

**Traceability** demands that every pricing decision can be traced back to its source data, including market benchmarks, condition assessments, and risk factors. This audit trail is essential for governance and dispute resolution.

---

## Impact

Since implementation, the standardized approach has contributed to more consistent appraisal outputs, faster dispute resolution (because evidence is readily available), and improved relationships with dealer partners who appreciate the transparency and professionalism of the documentation.
